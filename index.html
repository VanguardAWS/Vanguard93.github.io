US Navy Verteran

I was a naval aircrewman (AWS) for six years serving at helicopter squadron hm-14. Naval Aircrewman (Helicopter) (AWS) are members of multi-mission helicopter and Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) crews operating in maritime and overland environments; perform Search and Rescue (SAR), Airborne Mine Countermeasure (AMCM) Tactical Evacuations (TACEVAC), Humanitarian Assistance and Disaster Relief (HADR), Special Operations Forces (SOF) insertion and extraction, Personnel Recovery (PR), Direct Action (DA), Anti-Surface Warfare (ASUW), Intelligence Surveillance Reconnaissance (ISR), Logistics Support (LOG), aerial firefighting operations; operating Crew Served Weapons (CSW); and conduct observer duties for safety of flight.

Autonoumous Underwater Vehicle Operator

Once I left the Navy I worked for Oceaneering as an AUV (Autonomous Underwater Vehicle) Operator. As an AUV Operator I undertook essential maintenance and ensured that, when on a mission, the AUVs were able to operate as intended, whether that be collecting marine life data for scientific research, detecting underwater hazards for military operations, or surveying the seabed and ocean environment for commercial purposes. AUV operators also operate the AUV, to include mission planning, mission sensor/payload operations, launching, remotely piloting, and recovering the underwater vehicle.
